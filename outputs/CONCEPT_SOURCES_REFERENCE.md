## Case Studies (Learn by Example)

### Amazon
- **Kindle**: Working Backwards process (press release written before product)
- **AWS**: Customer obsession → Infrastructure as a Service
- **Prime**: Bundled value proposition → lock-in moat

### Google
- **Search**: Simplicity vs competition (Blue Ocean)
- **Gmail**: 1GB storage when competitors offered 2MB (RAISE factor)
- **Chrome**: Speed obsession → browser dominance

### Stripe
- **Developer-first**: API documentation as product
- **Pricing**: Usage-based → align with customer growth
- **Four-layer framework**: Payments → Optimization → Revenue Ops → Business Solutions

### Netflix
- **DVD → Streaming**: Market timing (broadband adoption)
- **Original Content**: Vertical integration moat
- **Culture Deck**: Context not control → empowered teams

### Airbnb
- **11-Star Experience**: Push beyond incremental improvements
- **Belonging Anywhere**: Emotional positioning vs "cheap hotels"
- **Host Community**: Two-sided marketplace with network effects

**Key Takeaway**: Study how world-class companies made strategic decisions, not just what they built.
