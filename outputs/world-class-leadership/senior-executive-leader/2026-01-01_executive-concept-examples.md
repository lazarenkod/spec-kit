## Example 1: B2B SaaS Platform Initiative

> **Scenario**: CPO pitching AI-powered analytics platform to Board for Series B funding

### Executive Summary

**TL;DR**: AI analytics platform targeting mid-market SaaS companies, enabling them to reduce churn by 30% and increase upsell revenue by 25%.

| Dimension | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Strategic Imperative** | Expand TAM beyond SMB to mid-market ($50M-$500M ARR segment) ‚Äî unlock 10x larger deals |
| **Market Opportunity** | $450M SOM over 3 years (5% of $9B TAM in SaaS analytics) |
| **Investment Required** | $12M over 18 months (Series B round) |
| **Expected Return** | $25M ARR by Year 3 ($75M valuation at 3x multiple) ‚Üí 6.2x ROI |
| **Risk Rating** | **Medium** ‚Äî Competitive market, requires ML expertise hire |
| **Decision Deadline** | Feb 15, 2026 ‚Äî Delay pushes launch to Q4 (miss seasonal buying window) |
| **Go/No-Go Criteria** | 10 design partners signed by June 2026; 60% show >20% churn reduction in pilot |

### Strategic Context

**Company OKR Alignment**:
- üéØ **Primary**: O1 "Expand to mid-market" ‚Üí This IS the vehicle for KR2 (5 mid-market logos by EOY)
- ‚ûï **Supporting**: O2 "Improve product margin" ‚Üí ML efficiency reduces support costs by 15%
- ‚è≥ **Future**: O3 "Build platform ecosystem" ‚Üí Analytics API unlocks 3rd-party integrations in 2027

**Strategic Fit Score**: **9/10** ‚Äî Direct alignment with growth strategy; only concern is team distraction from core product.

**Portfolio Context**:
- **Current Products**:
  - SMB Dashboard (mature, 80% gross margin, 5,000 customers)

- **This Initiative**: AI Analytics (new product line, adjacent TAM)

## Universal Executive Presentation Checklist

### Before Any Board/C-Suite Concept Review

**Pre-Meeting Checklist**:

- [ ] **One-Pager Sent 48 Hours Prior**: Executive Summary on single page, PDF
- [ ] **Financial Model Attached**: Excel with scenarios (best/base/worst)
- [ ] **Competitive Intel Current**: Checked competitor moves in last 30 days
- [ ] **Team Availability Confirmed**: Key hires identified, recruiting pipeline active
- [ ] **Risk Register Updated**: Top 3 risks with mitigations ready to discuss
- [ ] **Alternatives Documented**: 2-3 options considered, clear rationale for choice
- [ ] **Data Sources Cited**: Every market size/benchmark claim has footnote
- [ ] **Decision Request Clear**: "Asking for $X approval to proceed to Gate 1"

**Meeting Flow** (30-min format):

| Time | Section | Exec Focus | Common Objections |
|------|---------|------------|-------------------|
| **0-2 min** | Hook | "Why this matters to company strategy" | "Why now vs next quarter?" |
| **2-5 min** | Problem | "Market pain + our right to win" | "Are customers asking for this?" |
| **5-10 min** | Solution | "What we're building + differentiation" | "Can we partner instead of build?" |
| **10-15 min** | Business Case | "Investment, ROI, timeline" | "What's the payback period?" |
| **15-20 min** | Risks | "Top 3 risks + kill criteria" | "What if it fails ‚Äî how much do we lose?" |
| **20-25 min** | Q&A | *Anticipate questions below* | |
| **25-30 min** | Ask | "Approve $X for Gate 1 by [Date]" | "Let's see more customer validation first" |

**Top 10 Questions Every Executive Asks**:

1. **"Why now?"** ‚Üí Answer: Market timing, competitive urgency, internal readiness
2. **"Why us?"** ‚Üí Answer: Unique capability, customer trust, distribution advantage
3. **"What's the ROI?"** ‚Üí Answer: NPV, payback period, IRR with scenarios
4. **"What if it fails?"** ‚Üí Answer: Kill criteria, sunk cost cap, pivot options
5. **"Who else is doing this?"** ‚Üí Answer: Competitive landscape, our differentiation
6. **"Can we partner instead?"** ‚Üí Answer: Build vs buy analysis, why build wins
7. **"What do we stop doing?"** ‚Üí Answer: Explicit trade-offs, opportunity cost
8. **"How confident are you?"** ‚Üí Answer: Confidence level (%), validation plan
9. **"What could go wrong?"** ‚Üí Answer: Top 3 risks, mitigations, early warning signals
10. **"When do we decide to kill it?"** ‚Üí Answer: Stage-gates, metrics thresholds, decision authority

---

## Summary: What Makes Concepts "Executive-Ready"

### The 10 Commandments of Executive Concept Documents

1. **Lead with the Ask**: Decision request in first 30 seconds
2. **Show Strategic Alignment**: Explicit linkage to company OKRs/strategy
3. **Quantify Everything**: Market size, investment, ROI, risk ‚Äî all with numbers
4. **Present Alternatives**: Show what you're NOT doing and why
5. **Stage the Investment**: Tranched funding with gates, not all-or-nothing
6. **Define Kill Criteria**: Executives need off-ramps, not just go/go decisions
7. **Multi-Horizon Success**: 6mo validation, 1yr PMF, 3yr leadership goals
8. **Competitive Benchmarks**: Success relative to market, not absolute
9. **Team Sustainability**: Org health metrics, not just business metrics
10. **One-Page Summary**: If you can't explain it on one page, it's not ready

**Result**: Concepts that enable **confident, fast decisions** instead of endless debate.
