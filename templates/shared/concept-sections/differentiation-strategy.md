#### Our Approach

> **Instructions**: Explain HOW we solve this differently. Be specific with tactics, not vague aspirations.

**Strategic Approach** (75-125 words):
[Detailed explanation of unique solution with 3-5 specific tactics]

**Specific Tactics**:
1. **[Tactic 1]**: [1-2 sentence description with technical/business detail]
2. **[Tactic 2]**: [1-2 sentence description]
3. **[Tactic 3]**: [1-2 sentence description]
4. **[Tactic 4]**: [1-2 sentence description] (optional)
5. **[Tactic 5]**: [1-2 sentence description] (optional)

**Example**:

**Strategic Approach**:
We build a **multi-agent architecture** where specialist agents (Chief of Staff, Strategy, Operations, Communication, Decision) coordinate to handle 15-40% of executive decisions autonomously. Unlike chatbots that react to queries, our agents proactively monitor data streams, detect patterns, prepare briefings, and execute approved workflows—with human oversight for high-stakes decisions.

**Specific Tactics**:
1. **Chief of Staff Agent**: Orchestrates daily workflow—meeting prep with participant context, action item tracking, follow-up automation
2. **Strategy Agent**: Runs weekly competitive intel, scenario planning, strategic question prompts ("Have you considered X?")
3. **Decision Agent**: Applies learned frameworks to routine decisions (software purchases <$5K, PTO approvals), escalates complex ones
4. **Long-term Memory**: RAG-based system remembers past decisions, preferences, and outcomes to improve future recommendations
5. **Approval Rate Targeting**: 95%+ approval rate on autonomous actions monitored monthly; agents retrain if approval drops <90%

## Barriers to Entry & Defensibility

> **Instructions**: Explain why we can build these differentiators and why competitors can't/won't replicate them.

### Why We Can Win

**Key Barriers We're Building**:

1. **[Barrier Type 1]** (e.g., "Network Effects"): [How this moat strengthens over time]
   - [Specific mechanism creating barrier]
   - [Timeline to defensibility]: [X months/years]
   - [Evidence of moat potential]: [EV-XXX]

2. **[Barrier Type 2]** (e.g., "Data Moat"): [How data advantage compounds]
   - [Specific mechanism]
   - [Timeline]
   - [Evidence]

3. **[Barrier Type 3]** (e.g., "Expertise / Brand"): [How domain knowledge or brand creates switching costs]
   - [Specific mechanism]
   - [Timeline]
   - [Evidence]

**Example**:

1. **Network Effects**: First to build executive knowledge community with expertise effects
   - Template marketplace: Customers share decision frameworks, board templates, strategic playbooks
   - Peer benchmarking: Compare decision velocity vs. anonymous cohort
   - Timeline: 500+ templates and 1,000+ active sharers by Month 18
   - Evidence: Notion, Figma command 30-50% premium pricing with strong network effects [EV-052]

2. **Data Moat**: Unified semantic layer creates switching costs
   - Customers configure 50+ business system integrations—recreating elsewhere = 3-6 months effort
   - AI agents learn decision patterns over time—new system starts from zero
   - Timeline: Switching cost becomes prohibitive after 6 months of usage
   - Evidence: Average enterprise uses 367 apps (Gartner); integration complexity creates lock-in [EV-053]

3. **AI Learning**: Agents improve with usage, creating personalized moat
   - Decision approval rate improves from 80% (Month 1) to 95%+ (Month 6) through feedback loops
   - Each customer's agent becomes uniquely trained to their decision style
   - Timeline: Personalization moat reaches 95% within 6 months
   - Evidence: Reinforcement learning systems improve 15-25% per 10K decisions (OpenAI RLHF research) [EV-054]
