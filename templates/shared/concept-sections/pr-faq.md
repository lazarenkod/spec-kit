# Amazon Working Backwards: PR/FAQ

> **Purpose**: Define the customer experience before building by writing the launch press release and FAQs first. Forces clarity on value proposition and prevents feature bloat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Customer FAQs

**Q: Why is this better than [Primary Competitor]?**

A: [Primary Competitor] is built for [their target]. We're built specifically for [our target].
- [Specific difference 1]
- [Specific difference 2]
- [Quantified improvement: "X% faster/cheaper/easier"]

**Q: What if my team doesn't adopt it?**

A: [Adoption risk mitigation with evidence]
- [Adoption mechanism 1: e.g., "Works in tools they already use"]
- [Adoption metric: e.g., "90% daily usage within 2 weeks"]
- [Fallback: e.g., "Full refund if <50% adoption after 30 days"]

**Q: How long until I see results?**

A: [Realistic time-to-value]
- **Immediate**: [What they get on day 1]

- **Month 1**: [Full value realization]

**Q: What's the total cost of ownership?**

A: [Transparent pricing breakdown]
- Base cost: $[X]/[period]
- Implementation: [Hours/cost]
- Ongoing maintenance: [Minimal/hours per week]
- Hidden costs: [None/list any]

## PR/FAQ Quality Checklist

- [ ] Customer value clear in first paragraph
- [ ] Quantified benefits (not just features)
- [ ] Specific customer quote (feels real, not generic)
- [ ] Differentiation vs competitors explicit
- [ ] Pricing and availability concrete
- [ ] "Why now" answered with evidence
- [ ] "What we're NOT building" explicit
- [ ] Risks acknowledged with mitigations
- [ ] MVP scope defined
